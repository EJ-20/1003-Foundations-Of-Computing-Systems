Computer Science 1003
Regular Expressions and DFA Practice Exercises

 

Lots of exercises here to get you familiar with writing regular expressions and drawing deterministic finite automata.

When you have found the solutions, compare each regular expression with its corresponding DFA.  Notice how some complex expressions have comparatively equivalent DFA, and vice versa.

Note: There is usually more than one regular expression or DFA that describes a language, so consider the solutions given here as representative, rather than definitive.

 

Exercises

For each of the following languages:

a) Write a regular expression.

b) Draw a deterministic finite automaton.

 

1. Strings that only contain 0's.

a) Regular expression: 0*

b)

 

2. All possible binary strings.

a) Regular expression: (0|1)*

b)

 

3. All binary strings, except for the empty string.

a) Regular expression: (0|1)(0|1)* , or can write as (0|1)+

b)

 

4. String is either a or bb or bab.

a) Regular expression: a|bb|bab

b)

 

5. Strings are taken from the alphabet {x,y}, and begin with x and end with y.

a) Regular expression: x(x|y)*y

b)

 

6. Binary strings that end with 00.

a) Regular expression: (0|1)*00

b)

 

7. Strings are taken from the alphabet {s,t,u} and contain at least three t's:

a) Regular expression: ((s|u)*t){3}(s|u)*

b)

 

8. Strings are taken from the alphabet {s,t,u} and contain at least three consecutive t's:

a) Regular expression: (s|u)*ttt(s|u)* , or (s|u)*t{3}(s|u)*

b)

 

9. Binary strings that contain the substring 110.

a) Regular expression: (0|1)*110(0|1)*

b)

 

10. Binary strings that don't contain the substring 110.

a) Regular expression: (0|10)*1*

b)

 

11. Strings are taken from the alphabet {x,y}, and contain at least two x's, but not consecutive x's.

a) Regular expression: y*xyy*x(yy*x)*y*

b)

 

12. Strings are taken from the alphabet {a,b,c}, have length of at least 3 characters, and the third character is c.

a) Regular expression: ..c.* is simplest, or can write as (a|b|c){2}c(a|b|c)*

b)

 

13. Binary strings where the number of 0's is a multiple of 3.

a) Regular expression: ((1*0){3})*1*

b)

 

14. Strings are taken from the alphabet {a,b}, and start and end with the same character.

a) Regular expression: (a(b|a)*a)|(b(b|a)*b)

b)

 

15. Binary strings that start with 0 and have odd length, or start with 1 and have even length.

a) Regular expression: (0|1(0|1))(0|1){2})*

b)

 

Some of these exercises were adapted in part from questions at qatar.cmu.edu/cs/15212.
